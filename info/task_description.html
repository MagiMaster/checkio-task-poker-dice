You're at a boardgame convention, but you've gotten yourself in a situation. You registered for two tournaments, but later realized they both take place at the same time. Fortunately for you, you have a robotic double on hand to attend one of the tournaments. Now you just have to program it so it knows how to play the game.

The game in question involves rolling 5 poker dice. After a roll you can choose to keep some of the dice and reroll the others. (You can choose to "unkeep" dice between rolls.) After two rerolls, or less if you want, you must choose which category to place your score in. After all of the categories have been scored, you add everything up to determine your final score.

Poker dice are six-sided dice labeled with various cards. For this task, all the dice are the same and are labeled with the 9 of spades, 10 of hearts, jack of spades, queen of hearts, king of hearts and ace of spades.

To play the game, you are given a list of your previous rolls and a dictionary containing your current score for each category you've scored in. You must return a list of which dice to keep or a string containing the name of the category to score. If this is your third roll then you must choose a category. If you pick a category you do not qualify for, it is worth 0 points. You have eight hands per game. You can overwrite a score with a new roll, but doing so will mean you won't have enough hands to score in every category.

Each die is stored as a string containing the rank and suit as a single character each: "9S", "TH", "JS", "QH", "KH" or "AS". The category names must match those in the tables below except for capitalization (lower them).

The categories are:

Category Value
One Pair 2 (3 for a pair of aces)
Two Pair 4 (5 with a pair of aces)
Three of a Kind 6 (8 with 3 aces)
Flush 15
Straight 25
Full House 25 (30 with 3 aces)
Four of a Kind 50 (60 with 4 aces)
Five of a Kind 200 (240 with 5 aces)

This mission score are dynamical and will be calculated based on your solution results. You will play 5 games and earn a sum of points for these games. Each game start in a new environment.

Input: Two arguments. Your previous rolls as a list of lists and scored categories a dictionary.

Output: A list of dice to keep as a list/tuple of strings or the name of a category to score as a string.
